cmmnt

cmmnt is a web and mobile application for sharing and discussing links privately You can send a person or group a link to an article, video, tweet, or picture, and start a private conversation around it. There is also a chrome extension to make sending links easier.

cmmnt is built on a Ruby on Rails backend using Zurb Foundation. The chrome extension is just vanilla Javascript. The apps are both native (in Java and Objective-C), but use a hybrid of native navigation and webviews to lay out the content to speed up development time considerably.

Dorm Room Fund

I spend my senior year at BU investing in and helping student-led startups through Dorm Room Fund. DRF is a 500k fund backed by First Round Capital focused solely on students. I was on the first Boston team along with colleagues from other area schools.

After spending some time as an entrepreneur, I found it very valuable to get a perspective from the other side of the equation. I also really enjoyed the opportunity to help out my peers since I could really relate to what they were doing.

Credport

Credport was a company that I started with my good friend Nam during our sophomore year at BU. We were fascinated by the rise of marketplaces and collaborative consumption and believed that we could build a portable reputation to take around the web.

We took part in the Summer@Highland program with Highland Capital, and then were accepted into Startupbootcamp Berlin so we took a leave of absence during our junior year to move to Berlin and work on Credport. We talked to tons of marketplaces and released a product with a few partners, but then realized that we couldn't grow a userbase so we shut the company down and returned to school.

Late Night Safety Kit

At a hackathon, Battlehack Boston, I built a Late Night Safety Kit with a few friends. It is a mobile application which provides a few different tools for people to feel safe at night, including crime in the local area, a timer to contact people in case you don't make it home, and a tracker. We ended up winning the hackathon.

The app uses Cordova to access native functions on both iOS and Android, Ruby on Rails for the backend, and React.js, Backbone, and Zurb Foundation for the frontend.

Senior Design Project

In my senior year, I helped design and build an Arduino-powered LED array along with two classmates. The device was designed to sit under a 96 well plate and allow researchers to run synthetic biology experiments focused on optogenetics in the Khalil Lab at BU.

I used Altium Designer to design the printed circuit board which held all the LEDs and the circuit. It was connected to Arduino, which had a Bluetooth Low Energy module to communicate with an Android app.
